### To maintain and safekeep your passwords, you must...
Follow the guidelines below, that are strongly recommended by multiple safety and standards organisation across the world.

#### 👁️ Never expose your password
Don't keep your password unprotected or exposed such that they're completely visible through minimal efforts. Examples of such cases are:

- Notes and notebooks.
- Shared or visible screens/storage.
- Unencrypted files/storage.
- Unencrypted messages.
- Audibly spoken.

#### 👥 Use different passwords for different platforms
Using the same password for multiple or all platforms leads to indirect breach of them, even if only one of them was breached. Different passwords help prevent breaches outside the affected platform.

#### 💭 Avoid predictable patterns and personal topics
- Your favourite activity, place, person, memories, etc. are known to multiple people around you, who might've shared with others around them. Do not make them your password.
- If you use something like "`I_AM_the_youtube-K1nG`" for YouTube, don't use anything like "``I_AM_the_f4c3b00k-K1nG``" for Facebook.
- Randomise ALL passsowrds.

#### 🔄 Change them periodically
It is advised to change your passwords every 120 days. Earlier for more critical platforms, later for less critical ones.

#### 🫆 Enable MFA
Multi-Factor Authentication are a set of unique and independent key items that are required simultaneously for full authentication.

Use at least 2 of the following authentication factor categories:

- _Something **you are**_ (face, iris/retina, fingerprint).
- _Something **you know**_ (passwords, codes, patterns, etc.).
- _Something **you have**_ (mobile device, USB key, key cards, etc.).

#### 🗃️ Use a Password Manager
Password Managers are simply a database of your passwords/credentials for each platform, locked behind one strong and secure password set by the user. The user has to remember only _one_ password for retrieving the credentials for all of their platforms.

Almost all password managers can generate a completely random password following the usual security guidelines. Password Managers that run as browser extensions, can automatically detect the platform from the webpage and apply the credentials without any user-input other than the master password. Most of them also provide cloud-features to ease configuration across multiple devices for a user. Some of them also offer other authentication methods, like scanning fingerprint, face, USB key, key cards.

To choose the best Password Manager for you,

- Identify the features you need the most.
- Weigh the pros and cons of their ecosystem, like open or closed source, first-party cloud or self-hosted, browser extension or dedicated app, etc.
- Check their history for breaches, exploits and vulnerabilities.
- Read user-reviews and audit reports from multiple different and independent sources.
